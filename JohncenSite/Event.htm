“Chinese Literature and Culture”

-Talk by Prof. Göran Malmqvist

speaker: Professor Göran Malmqvist
Time: 16:00-17:30, 5 February 2015 (Thursday)
Fee: registration required
Venue: informed by email after registration
Organiser: Cambridge China Development Forum (CCDF)
Registration link:
https://docs.google.com/forms/d/19DmE0ybTCE72wVGMHJQ3uJsd8m03WfKaNdxpxFPk_20/viewform
On 5 February 2015 (Thursday), Prof. Göran Malmqvist is going to give us a talk on his research experience as a sinologist. In this talk, Professor Malmqvist would dwell on his early career as a sinologist, the conditions of sinological research in the late 1940s, his activities as a translator of Chinese literature, Ancient, Medieval, Modern and Contemporary, and perhaps also include a few anecdotes about some of the strange people he had met during his sojourns in China.

Professor Göran Malmqvist (born 6 June 1924) is a Swedish linguist, member of the Swedish Academy (Chair No.5), literary historian, sinologist and translator. He studied Chinese literature under Professor Bernhard Karlgren at Stockholm University. He has played a critical role in introducing Chinese literature to the West. He is a member of The Nobel Committee for Literature.
